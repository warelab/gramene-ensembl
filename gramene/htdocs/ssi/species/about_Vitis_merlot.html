About Vitis vinifera L. ssp. vinifera cv. Merlot clone FPS15

Merlot is a hermaphrodite Vv vinifera cultivar, one of the primary grapes used in Bordeaux wine, and the most widely planted grape in the Bordeaux wine regions. It is an offspring of Cabernet Franc and a half-sibling of Carménère, Malbec and Cabernet Sauvignon (Boursiquot et al, 2009).

Assembly

Genome assembly of Vv vinifera cv. Merlot was performed at DNAnexus (Mountain View,

CA, USA). Repetitive regions were marked using the TANmask and REPmask modules

from the DAmasker4 before and after error correction of the SMRT reads. Then, error-corrected

reads were assembled with FALCON v.1.7.75 and haplotype phasing was performed using FALCON-Unzip with parameters described in Massonnet et al (2020). The primary PacBio assembly (64X coverage) is 606.1 Mbp in length with 1,483 contigs and N50 = 811 Kbp.

Annotation

Genome annotation was performed by Massonnet et al (2020) as described previously in Vondras et al (2019). RepeatMasker was used to identify and mask repetitive elements prior to gene prediction. Protein-coding genes were predicted using EVidenceModeler. Ab initio prediction was performed with several predictors (SNAP, GeneMark-ES, GlimmerHMM, GeneID) trained on Cabernet Sauvignon, and Augustus trained with a BUSCO dataset. Next, gene models were obtained with PASA and by using Vitis ESTs and flcDNAs (downloaded from GenBank), Vv vinifera PN40024 V1 coding sequences (CDS; http://genomes.cribi.unipd.it/DATA/), Vv vinifera cv. Tannat (TSA GAKH01.1) and Vv vinifera cv. Corvina (TSA PRJNA169607) transcriptomes, and Cabernet Sauvignon corrected Iso-Seq reads (SRP132320) as transcriptional evidence. For each genotype, RNA-seq samples were trimmed using Trimmomatic. EVidenceModeler was used to integrate the ab initio predictions and PASA gene models with experimental evidence, which included proteins from Swissprot viridiplantae. Models featuring in-frame stop codons were removed. Functional annotations of the encoded proteins were assigned based on sequence homology with proteins from the RefSeq plant protein database and domain identification by InterProScan. Gene locus nomenclature was adapted from Grimplet et al (2014).
