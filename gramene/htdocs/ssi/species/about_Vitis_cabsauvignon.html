About Vitis vinifera L. ssp. vinifera cv. Cabernet Sauvignon clone FPS 08

V. vinifera cv. Cabernet Sauvignon is one of the most widely cultivated wine grape cultivars, and was produced from a cross between two very distinct cultivars, Cabernet Franc and Sauvignon Blanc. Whether a spontaneous hybrid or a product of human breeding, all of the Cabernet Sauvignon grown around the world is thought to have resulted from this single hybridization event, estimated to have taken place sometime before the 17th century in the Aquitaine region of France (Bowers and Meredith, 1997).

Assembly

The genome of the Cabernet Sauvignon clone 08 at Foundation Plant Services (UC Davis) was sequenced with a coverage depth of ~140x using SMRT sequencing technology (Chin et al, 2016). Sequencing reads were then assembled using FALCON-unzip into a highly contiguous genome that integrated phased haplotype information. FALCON-unzip generated a set of primary contigs (591.4 Mbp in 718 contigs with N50 = 2.17 Mbp) that covers one of the two haplotypes, and a set of correlated haplotigs (367.8 Mbp in 2,037 contigs with N50 = 0.80 Mbp). For additional details, see Chin et al (2016).

The assembly was further improved with HiC proximity-based ligation, optical mapping, and multiple scaffolding methods, resulting in two phased copies, hap1 and hap2, of all 19 chromosomes (Massonnet et al, 2020).

Annotation

Genome annotation was performed by Massonnet et al (2020) as described previously in Vondras et al (2019). RepeatMasker was used to identify and mask repetitive elements prior to gene prediction. Protein-coding genes were predicted using EVidenceModeler. Ab initio prediction was performed with several predictors (SNAP, GeneMark-ES, GlimmerHMM, GeneID) trained on Cabernet Sauvignon, and Augustus trained with a BUSCO dataset. Next, gene models were obtained with PASA and by using Vitis ESTs and flcDNAs (downloaded from GenBank), Vv vinifera PN40024 V1 coding sequences (CDS; http://genomes.cribi.unipd.it/DATA/), Vv vinifera cv. Tannat (TSA GAKH01.1) and Vv vinifera cv. Corvina (TSA PRJNA169607) transcriptomes, and Cabernet Sauvignon corrected Iso-Seq reads (SRP132320) as transcriptional evidence. For each genotype, RNA-seq samples were trimmed using Trimmomatic. EVidenceModeler was used to integrate the ab initio predictions and PASA gene models with experimental evidence, which included proteins from Swissprot viridiplantae. Models featuring in-frame stop codons were removed. Functional annotations of the encoded proteins were assigned based on sequence homology with proteins from the RefSeq plant protein database and domain identification by InterProScan. For each genome, gene locus nomenclature was adapted from Grimplet et al (2014).
