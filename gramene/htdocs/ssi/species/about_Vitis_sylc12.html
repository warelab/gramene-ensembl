About Vinis vinifera L. ssp. sylvestris C1-2

Vitis vinifera ssp. sylvestris (V. sylvestris) is the wild progenitor of domesticated and cultivated grapevine Vitis vinifera ssp. vinifera (V. vinifera). Sylvestris C1-2 with plant code 8500.Col.C1-2, has its origins in the geographic location of Sainte-Croix-en-Plaine, Haut-Rhin, France. Contrary to domesticated V. vinifera plants, which are hermaphrodite, V. sylvestris plants are dioecious, meaning that male and female reproductive organs are in separate individuals. The V. sylvestris reference genome herein described for clone C1-2 (Badoin et al, 2020) corresponds to a female individual.

Assembly

Vinis vinifera L. ssp. Sylvestris C1-2 was sequenced on an PacBio sequencer using SMRT-sequencing (120X). Contigs were assembled with Falcon-integrate (Falcon + Falcon-unzip), in order to obtain a diploid assembly (the two haplotypes). With Falcon, PacBio reads were self-corrected, assembly was performed, haplotypes were generated, assembly was polished with Arrow, and finally a phased diploid assembly was obtained, as described in Badoin et al (2020). The total length of the high-quality assembly was 469 Mbp with 591 contigs and a contig N50 of 1.7 Mbp.

Annotation

RNA Illumina paired-end reads obtained from grape berries of Sylvestris C1-2 were used to assemble a transcriptome in order to annotate the genome (Badoin et al, 2020). The transcriptome was assembled using Trinity (Grabherr et al, 2011) resulting in 398,189 putative transcripts (422,652,106 bp) with a N50 of 2217 bp. Using an experimental evidence-based approach, two gene annotations were produced, one for primary contigs (39,031 annotated protein-coding genes) and one for haplotigs. For additional details, please see Badoin et al (2020).
