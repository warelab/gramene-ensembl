About Vitis vinifera L. ssp. Vinifera cv. Carménère clone FPS 02

Carménère (also known as Grand Vidure) is a historically and economically important wine grape cultivar. It is an old French cultivar, thought to be derived from a cross between Cabernet Franc and Gros Cabernet (Boursiquot et al. 2009). It was widely planted in the Bordeaux regions of Graves and Médoc before the aphid-like soil-born pest Phylloxera vastatrix devastated French vineyards in the 19th century. While almost extinct in France due to poor fruit set and late ripening, Carménère was brought to Chile in the 1850s with other Bordeaux grapes just before phylloxera hit Europe, and has adapted well to the Chilean climate and soil where it has become the flagship red wine grape (Servicio Agrícola y Ganadero, https://www.sag.gob.cl, 2016). Assembly

The genome of Vitis vinifera cv. Carménère clone 02 (VCR702) was sequenced at 115X coverage using Single Molecule Real Time (SMRT; Pacific Biosciences) technology. The long reads (N50 = 13.1 Kbp) were assembled into primary contigs and haplotigs using the diploid-aware assembler FALCON-Unzip, a diploid-aware assembly pipeline (Chin et al. 2016). The primary assembly consisted of 1,411 scaffolds covering 622.8 Mbp (N50 = 1.04 Mbp) with a maximum length of 5.9 Mbp and as few as 857 Kbp in gaps (0.14%). The haplotigs were more fragmented and covered only 420.3 Mbp (7,969 contigs, N50 = 89.6 Kbp). For more detailed information please see Minio et al (2019).

Annotation

Protein-coding genes were predicted with EVidenceModeler (v.1.1.1; Haas et al. 2008) using as input both predicted gene models such as ab initio and PASA predictions based on Vitis transcriptional evidence; as well as proteins from Swissprot viridiplantae, Vitis ESTs, flcDNAs, CDS, Iso-Seq reads, and transcriptomes. Repetitive sequences were identified with RepeatMasker (v.open-4.0.6; Smit et al. 2013) and masked prior to prediction. A total of 73,109 protein-coding genes were found in the assembly, 40,684 in the primary assembly, and 32,425 in the haplotigs. Functional annotations were assigned based on homology with RefSeq plant proteins and on functional domains identified with InterProScan (v.5; Jones et al, 2014). Read more in Minio et al (2019).
